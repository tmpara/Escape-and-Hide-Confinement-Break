# Escape & Hide: Confinement Break

Escape & Hide: Confinement Break is a game project built with Angular. It's a turn-based (maybe multiplayer) survival roguelike experience about trying to flee the facility while avoiding or fighting enemy humans and monsters.

## Features
- Turn-based combat and gameplay
    - Players and enemies will have a turn during which they can perform their actions
    - All of a team moves at the same time, using energy to limit the they can perform actions
- Procedural world generation
    - Map will consist of varying amount of rooms
    - Each room will be randomly generated
    - Points of interests that will generate throughout the map
    - Players will move between rooms if everyone agrees to
- Advanced health system using blood
    - Players will have a different amount of blood at the start, ranging between 4 and 6 liters
    - Amount of blood loss will depend on the type of wound
    - Blood amount will affect the players abilities to perform actions
- Unique genetic system allowing players to modify their genes to gain various buffs or debuffs
    - Player can scavenge the map and fallen enemies for genetic materials
    - Special rooms and items will exist to allow the player to modify their genes
    - A minigame will exist to perform genetic modifications
    - The player will gain modifiers depending on genes they modify themself with
- Internal organs system
    - Players and most enemies will have organs as part of this system
    - Organs can fail, granting various debuffs or leading to death
    - This includes brain, heart, liver, lungs, kidneys, stomach, bones
    - Players have the ability to replace their organs to lower/remove the debuffs or avoid death
- Various environmental hazards
    - The world will be full of hazards like fire, smoke, radiation and various anomalies
    - Fire can set things on fire, being able to kill players, enemies and damage the map
    - Smoke can obstruct vision, allowing for stealthy movement or surprise fights
    - Radiation will induce radiation sickness, killing the player over time if not treated
    - Anomalies, which can summon unique enemies, disable objects, teleport the player and such
- Unique enemies to fight
    - Human enemies like scientists working at the place and security guards attempting to lock it down and prevent escape
    - Human-like test subjects who were victims of mostly various experiments, having different abilities from normal humans
    - Former human failed test subjects who were victims of various experiments, making them either easy or hard to defeat with unique abilities
    - Anomaly spawned enemies which aren't like any other enemy, with dangerous and unique abilities
- Per turn  energy management
    - Players have limited actions for every turn, depending on the amount of energy they have
    - Every action performed drains their energy
    - When player runs out of energy, they can't perform actions anymore
    - Enemies will have a similar system to limit their movement and actions
- Inventory system for item management
    - Players will have an inventory to allow keeping items they find around the map
    - Inventory space will (maybe) vary depending on their genes and items they find
    - Equipped items limit will never change
    - Equipped items will be usable during combat and general actions to fight or help teammates
- Big variety of items to help the player survive
    - Players could be able to find random items around the map to help them during the game
    - It will include items such as weapons, healing items, genetic materials, generic and potentially useful loot
- Deep lore of the playable world
    - Elements of lore put around the map helping the player understand the history of the map
    - It will help the player make decisions during the game
    - Player will gain more understanding of their current situation
